**It is not magnetic. It is vibrational.**

## Scientific basis

Studies by the National Research Council of Italy (CNR) have confirmed that every cell in the human body vibrates at defined frequencies, generating its own "sound signature". Healthy and damaged cells have different frequencies. Pioneering experiments (1981) demonstrated that human blood cells change color and shape in response to specific sound frequencies.

Research from the American Academy of Neurology (2019) showed that controlled vibration affects cell proliferation: certain frequencies (20, 200, 20000 Hz) increase proliferation, while others (2000 Hz) decrease it. A study published in Nature (2021) demonstrated that nanometric vibrations (1000 Hz, 40 nm amplitude) reduce the formation of bone-degrading cells and increase new bone formation.

## Premise

If we can measure the frequency of a damaged cell and apply the frequency that restores its natural state, we could induce its regeneration.

## What it does

The system uses two existing certified devices:

1. **Certified human vibrometer** (PCE-VM 31 or Pulsar vB²) — measures the actual vibration of the affected area. Compliant with ISO 5349 (hand-arm) and ISO 2631 (whole body).

2. **Certified medical stimulator** (Vibra 3.0 or similar) — applies focal vibration to the affected area, with frequencies of 30–900 Hz, pressure >350 mbar, up to 29 independent outputs.

The project does not manufacture hardware. It integrates existing certified equipment and defines the usage protocol for cellular restoration.
